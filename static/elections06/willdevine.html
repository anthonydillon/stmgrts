Background

Tell us about your education?

Born and educated in Glasgow. Joined the Metropolitan Police in London from 1981 to 1988 and left at the rank of Sergeant to study for the Catholic Priesthood.

Went to London university and graduated in Theology.

What are three jobs you have held?

  1. Psychodynamic Counsellor and Organisaional Trauma Practitioner (present).
  2. Worked as a counselor in the NHS in the field of HIV & Sexual Health.
  3. Trainee for the Priesthood and Metropolotan Police Sergeant.

That’s 4 jobs.

What are you links to the North Twickenham/St Margarets community?

I live in Saint Margarets and have lived in the area for the last couple of years, East Twickenham just over a year ago.

Why did/do you want to be a councilor?

I’d like to build on and develop community relations, I’d like to develop understanding across sections of the community, to help appreciate diversity and assist North Twickenham being a safer, happier and more community based place to live and grow.

Issues

What do you think are the top three issues for North Twickenham/St Margarets and what do you should be done about them?

  1. Good value public services e.g. in education, welfare, facilities, amenities, safe streets and pavements, cleaner areas.
  2. Fostering a culture of entrepreneurialism and collaboration across local businesses and the community.
  3. Developing community links, bonds, understanding and appreciation across sections of the community: a sense of safety and enjoyment in living in the community.

What areas are you passionate about and what committees would you like to sit on?

  1. Entrepreneurialism and therefore community based commerce.
  2. Developing deeply understood community relations and celebrating diversity.

Are there any big things you are looking to achieve or change?

Working on the above areas would be big achievements.

If elected, how will you keep in-touch with your constituents?

Meet them via various media, be accessible, listen and respond. I’d develop a collaborative team: I can’t do all of this alone. Draw on the resources of my colleagues in the local Labour Party.
