Background

Tell us about your education?

St. Agnes, Isles of Scilly primary school, then Truro High School, Cornwall, then an MA European Politics, Brunel University.

What are three jobs you have held?

  1. Present – Chair of Richmond Environmental Information Centre (Voluntary)
  2. 2000/2002 – GLA, Research & Support Officer for Darren Johnson AM (Green Assembly Member)
  3. 1998/2000 - London Green Party, Election Campaign Manager

What are you links to the North Twickenham/St Margarets community?

Lived in St. Margarets for 27 years (Richmond for ten years before that); children went to Maria Grey Nursery, Orleans Infants, St. Stephens, Waldegrave and then Richmond College. I worked for West London Institute and then Brunel University in St. Margarets. Now active with North St. Margarets Residents’ Association

Why did/do you want to be a councilor?

To give local people a committed representative on the Council, and to give Green issues a strong voice locally

Issues

What do you think are the top three issues for North Twickenham/St Margarets and what do you should be done about them?

  1. Community – without a doubt the most important issue, from which all others stem
  2. Local schools should be available for local children so that primary school children can attend their community school with their friends and without having to travel by car
  3. Local businesses – support should be given to help local businesses survive the onslaught of the Superdrugs and Tescos.

What areas are you passionate about and what committees would you like to sit on?

Planning, there is so much potential for good planning and, unfortunately some opportunities for abuse.

Also, Education – I’m still not convinced that the need for a local primary school has diminished

Are there any big things you are looking to achieve or change?

Unfortunately even the majority party is unable to change much in four years, but I will certainly give the leading administration a headache as I rattle cages for the community, e.g. on a local school for primary children; the Mogden pong; Heathrow 3rd runway; support for local shops and businesses

If elected, how will you keep in-touch with your constituents?

I will hold regular surgeries; I will visit local people and shopkeepers; I will always be accessible by e-mail and phone
