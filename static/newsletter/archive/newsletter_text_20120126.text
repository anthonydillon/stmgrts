﻿DISORDER ON THE BORDER
----------------------------------------------------------------------

    "A good fence helpeth to keepe peace between neighbours; but let vs
     take heed that we make not a high stone wall, to keepe vs
     from meeting."

    -- 1640 E. Rogers Letter in Winthrop Papers

    The best of neighbours are not always the best of friends and so it
    was with Twickenham, Isleworth and Heston. For over 1,000 years
    they have been squabbling about something or other.

    In the 7th century. Theodore, the 8th Archbishop of Canterbury, in
    a move to reform the fledging church, introduced a system
    of parishes run by a parish priest. For us this meant the parish
    of Isleworth, later subdivided into the parishes of Heston,
    Isleworth and Twickenham. All would have been well were it not
    for the tithes, the payment each church collected within its
    parish. The larger the parish the more tithes the church could
    collect… and where there's tithes there's trouble!
