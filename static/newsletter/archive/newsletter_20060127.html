Latest News

Tesco Refused Permission for Advertising Signage — Again

The Richmond Council refused Tesco’s second request for its advertising signage. The council felt that second application provided no material changes to the already refused first proposal, they only attempted to prove that other St Margarets shops were using similar materials. So the Council refused this planning request on same grounds as the first. The Decision Notice states,

“The signage, by reason of its design, length and materials, is inappropriate to the age and character of the existing shopfront and determental to the appearance and character of this Building of Townscape Merit and the Convervation Area in general.”

English Heritage’s Licence for Marble Hill

English Heritage has applied for a new license to serve alcohol at Marble Hill Park from 10:30 am to 11 pm, Monday through Sunday and to 1:30 pm for weddings, special functions and events. The period of commenting on the application is closed.

This application makes sense considering the recent approval to hold weddings at the house, the annual concerts and the still undecided planning request to setup marquees for up to 16 events per year.

After numerous groups contacted him to protest the licensing request, Local MP, Vincent Cable has contacted English Heritage.
